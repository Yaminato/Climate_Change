Understanding Climate Change

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather patterns. While climate change is a natural phenomenon, human activities have been the primary driver since the 1800s, primarily due to the burning of fossil fuels.

What Causes Climate Change?

The main causes of current climate change include:

  • Burning Fossil Fuels: Coal, oil, and gas release greenhouse gases that trap heat in the atmosphere
  • Deforestation: Trees absorb CO2, so cutting them down reduces nature's ability to regulate the atmosphere
  • Industrial Activities: Manufacturing and industrial processes release significant amounts of greenhouse gases
  • Agriculture: Livestock farming produces methane, a potent greenhouse gas

What Can We Do?

Everyone can contribute to fighting climate change:

  • Reduce energy consumption and switch to renewable energy sources
  • Use public transportation, bike, or walk instead of driving
  • Reduce, reuse, and recycle to minimize waste
  • Support sustainable and local products
  • Plant trees and support reforestation efforts
  • Educate others about climate change
  • Advocate for climate policies and regulations
